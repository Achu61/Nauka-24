eFootball Rules

Closing Date:

Report at the venue of the event before 30 minutes.

It is an individual competition.

Match Type: Standard

Match Time: 8 minutes

Maximum Substitutes: 5

Players' Condition: Must be set to random

Connection and Aggregate Rules:

  1. If a match disconnects:

    • Within the first 15 minutes or below: Resume and play additional 90 minutes as aggregate.
    • Between 15-30 minutes: Play an additional 75 minutes.
    • Between 30-45 minutes: Play an additional 60 minutes.
    • Between 45-60 minutes: Play the remaining 45 minutes (second half).
    • Between 60-75 minutes: Play an additional 30 minutes.
    • Between 75-90 minutes: Play an additional 15 minutes.

    All scenarios require playing an aggregate match.

  2. If the game lags while aiming for a goal:

    • The goal counts if the player can prove the attempt through video recording or a clear screenshot showing the graph undershot on the screen.

The rules mention proof required for goal chances, which include a video record or a clear screenshot of the undershot graph on the screen.

In case of a lag during a goal attempt, proof from the player showing the attempt is necessary for the goal to be valid.

Chess Rules

Colseing Date:

Must come with College ID/School ID/Government approved ID proof.

Report at the venue of the event before 30 minutes.

It is an individual competition.

TOUCH MOVE:

When castling, the king shall be touched and moved first. Players will receive a warning for the first violation of this rule. Thereafter, if the rook is touched before the king, castling is not allowed and the rook must move (if legally possible).

CLOCKS:

For groups using chess clocks, the time control will be announced at the beginning of the tournament. Time controls are either 15 minutes with 5-second delay, or 20 minutes with 5-second delay.

The tournament director may, at his discretion,

Players must make their move and press the clock with the same hand.

WON GAMES:

A game is won:

  1. By checkmate;
  2. If the opponent resigns;
  3. If the opponent runs out of time, provided the following conditions are met: To claim a win on time, a player must notify a tournament official and have mating material. Claiming a win on time is invalid after a checkmate or stalemate has been played, or after a player has resigned or agreed to a draw.

DRAWN GAMES:

A game is drawn:

  1. By stalemate;
  2. By agreement of the players during the game;
  3. By three-fold repetition
  4. By fifty-move rule.

Maximum number of players :-16
